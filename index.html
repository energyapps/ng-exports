U.S. Natural Gas Exports Continue Record Growth

The volume of natural gas exports increased for the fourth consecutive year in 2018

The United States became a net natural gas exporter on an annual basis—for the first time in nearly 60 years—in 2017 and continued to export more natural gas than it imported in 2018. U.S. total natural gas exports grew 14% in 2018, and liquefied natural gas (LNG) exports grew by 53%.

The transition to net exporter occurred as U.S. natural gas production continued to grow, reducing pipeline imports from Canada and increasing exports both by pipeline and as LNG. EIA projects continued increases in U.S. LNG export capacity will make it the world’s third largest LNG exporter by the end of 2019, behind Australia and Qatar.
