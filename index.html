U.S. Natural Gas Exports Continue Record Growth

Export capacity of liquefied natural gas is expected to more than double by the end of 2019

The United States began exporting liquefied natural gas (LNG) from the Lower 48 states in February 2016, reflecting a decade of natural gas production growth and the development of new LNG export facilities. LNG is natural gas that has been cooled to a liquid state so it can be shipped and stored. Increases in both natural gas production and liquefaction capacity helped make the U.S. a net exporter of natural gas on an average annual basis for the first time in nearly six decades.
